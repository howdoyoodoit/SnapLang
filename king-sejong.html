The Sage King who gave Korea its voice.

Who was King Sejong?

King Sejong the Great (1397–1450) was the fourth monarch of the Joseon Dynasty of Korea. He is widely considered one of the greatest leaders in Korean history, known for his deep commitment to the welfare of his people and his immense contributions to science, technology, and culture.

His reign is often referred to as the "Golden Age" of Korean history, a period of unprecedented scientific and cultural flowering.

Standard portrait of King Sejong.
"The language of our people is different from that of the Chinese and does not match the Chinese characters. Therefore, even if the ignorant people want to communicate, many of them in the end cannot state their concerns." — King Sejong

The Creation of Hangul

Before the 15th century, Koreans wrote using Classical Chinese characters (Hanja). However, Hanja was extremely difficult to learn, meaning that only the elite upper class was literate. King Sejong felt deep sympathy for the common people who could not read or write.

A page from the Hunminjeongeum, the document that introduced Hangul in 1446.

In 1443, he personally led the creation of Hangul, a phonetic writing system designed specifically for the Korean language. Unlike Hanja, which is logographic, Hangul is an alphabetic system where the shapes of the consonants are based on the shape of the mouth and tongue when pronouncing them.

Scientific and Cultural Legacy

Beyond Hangul, King Sejong's reign saw incredible advancements in science and technology. He supported the work of Jang Yeong-sil, a brilliant scientist born into a low social class.

The Cheugugi, the world's first standardized rain gauge.
  • Astronomy: He commissioned the creation of celestial globes and water clocks.
  • Agriculture: He published Nongsa Jikseol, a book on farming techniques tailored to Korea's climate.
  • Medicine: He oversaw the compilation of an 85-volume encyclopedia of traditional Korean medicine.

Today, King Sejong is honored on the 10,000 KRW banknote, and his birthday is a major inspiration for Hangeul Day, celebrated on October 9th in South Korea.
